Board Briefs
 
March 2025
 
 
 
 
 
Full Marks to the Heads of Institutions
 
 

The United Board Heads of Institution Leadership Strategy Institute was inaugurated with a face-to-face summit held from 15 July to 19 July 2024 at Singapore Management University (SMU). This landmark gathering brought together 27 presidents, rectors, and principals from six South and Southeast Asian countries. The summit featured dynamic sessions on trends in higher education, adaptability, and leadership strategies, setting the stage for a transformative journey.

Designed as a multi-phase learning journey, the Leadership Strategy Institute equipped participants with tools and frameworks to drive impactful initiatives at their respective institutions. Following the onsite summit, participants transitioned into a practical application phase through a series of webinars. Over the next five months, they implemented leadership initiatives within their institutions, guided by insights gained during the summit.

On 11 November, a webinar brought together 21 participants to share reflections on their integration of strategic leadership principles into their institutional contexts. Discussions revolved around key questions: What were their takeaways from the summit? What experimental initiatives had they launched? What processes and results emerged from these experimentations? And finally, what lessons had they learned and what future plans had they devised?

Participants initiated a diverse range of innovative projects spanning education, leadership, finance, and technology. These included developing interdisciplinary courses and executive training programs to enhance academic offerings and professional development. Leadership pipelines were fortified through structured faculty development programs aimed at cultivating future leaders. Financial strategies were optimized by introducing new budgeting methods and repurposing underutilized assets to generate revenue. Additionally, some institutions embraced digital transformation by implementing enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems and automation technologies to streamline operations.
 
 

The implementation process followed several critical steps. Institutions began with comprehensive needs assessments to identify areas requiring intervention. They engaged key stakeholders such as faculty members, deans, students, industry collaborators, and government agencies to ensure buy-in and support. Challenges such as resistance to change and resource limitations arose but were addressed through strategies like structured training programs, phased implementation approaches, and financial planning.

The early results of these experimentations have been promising. Institutions reported improved collaboration among stakeholders and the successful introduction of new academic programs and IT infrastructure. Financial processes became more transparent, while international partnerships were strengthened through collaborative initiatives. These achievements not only bolstered leadership confidence but also reinforced mission-driven decision-making within participating institutions.

The successful completion of the Heads of Institution Leadership Strategy Institute stands as a testament to the dedication and enthusiasm of the participating presidents, rectors, and principals who invested their time and expertise in this initiative. Their story is one of collaboration, adaptability, and innovation in addressing complex challenges at the highest levels of higher education.
 
 

The institute also provided an invaluable opportunity for participants to interact with one another—an experience that fostered meaningful relationships and networks among leaders across Asia's higher education landscape. These connections are crucial for advancing higher education in the region as they encourage collaboration, facilitate the exchange of ideas, and inspire innovative solutions to shared challenges.

By empowering leaders with actionable insights and fostering a culture of cooperation across institutions, the Institute has laid a strong foundation for impactful leadership across Asia's higher education landscape. It is clear that these leaders are well-prepared to drive meaningful change through innovation, foresight, and collaboration—paving the way for a brighter future in higher education.
