Board Briefs
 
March 2025
 
 
 

Dear Trustees,

It is a pleasure to share the latest updates and highlights in this edition of Board Briefs. As we reflect on recent activities and prepare for the opportunities ahead, I want to begin by expressing my deep gratitude for your continued dedication and engagement in advancing the mission of the United Board.

Last weekend, we convened an intensive two-day Board Meeting on strategic planning, bringing together over 35 trustees (including trustees-elect), selected staff members, and Nygren Consulting. The gathering was a powerful demonstration of our collective commitment of our community to shaping a clear and impactful vision for the future. Your insights, thoughtful contributions, and collaborative spirit were invaluable. I extend my heartfelt thanks to each of you for your active participation, and to the Strategic Planning Steering Task Force for leading this critical initiative.

This meeting marks a significant step forward in charting a path that aligns with our shared aspirations for expanding our footprint and strengthening Christian higher education in Asia. The data and ideas generated over the weekend and after will be carefully analyzed by Dr. David Nygren and his team, forming the foundation for further discussions at our June Board Meeting in Thailand. I look forward to continuing this important journey together with your support.

This edition also highlights several key initiatives that reflect our mission in action.

  • The Heads of Institution Leadership Strategy Institute: Launched at Singapore Management University last July, this institute has already begun yielding transformative results. Even before the institute concluded with its final webinar in December, participating leaders had implemented innovative projects at their institutions with promising outcomes. These efforts illustrate the power of strategic leadership in driving meaningful change across higher education.
  • Expanding engagement in Northeast India: We recently conducted a series of leadership programs, institutional visits, and workshops that brought together educators and administrators from previously underserved regions. Highlights included faculty development sessions in Shillong, equipping participants with Whole Person Education (WPE) tools, and leadership workshops tailored to regional needs. Visits to institutions like Don Bosco College in Manipur deepened our engagement with local communities, fostering collaboration and innovation.
  • Strengthening alumni networks: We recently hosted an alumni gathering in Chennai that reunited 38 alumni from various United Board programs. This event not only reconnected alumni with one another and the United Board but also led to meaningful discussions on how their experiences have shaped their professional journeys. Their continued commitment to Whole Person Education underscores the multiplying impact of our work across Asia.
  • Deepening regional partnerships: Our February visits to Cambodia and Laos reinforced relationships with institutions such as the Royal University of Phnom Penh, Saint Paul Institute, and Pannasastra University, all in Cambodia, and Souphanouvong University while exploring new avenues for collaboration. These engagements highlighted both challenges—such as declining enrolment in Laos—and opportunities, including leadership development and faculty capacity-building programs that align with our mission.

Finally, I encourage you to review the details on upcoming committee meetings, and preparations for the June Board Meeting. I look forward to seeing you on Zoom and in person in Thailand and continuing our work together.

Thank you, once again, for your unwavering support and commitment. Together, we are strengthening the foundation for Whole Person Education and leadership development across Asia.

 

With gratitude and appreciation,

 
 

Pareena Gupta Lawrence
President
United Board for Christian Higher Education in Asia

Reflections on Staff Development
 
 

The staff development program facilitated by Open Consulting has been a transformative experience for staff, focusing on personal leadership, team dynamics and trust-building through guided coaching. Ricky and Taeko reflected on the journey:

 

Ricky Cheng, Executive Vice President

“My experience with Open Consulting, guided by the exceptional consultants Vivek, Sangeeta, and Ruby, has been transformative in both my professional and personal life. Their approach to team dynamics and trust-building exercises has left a lasting impact on our organization and its staff. The sessions were meticulously designed to foster an environment where biases are set aside, and trust is built through genuine connection and understanding. Through engaging and interactive exercises such as ‘empathy walk’ and the concept of ‘downloading’, we were encouraged to look beyond interactions on the surface and truly understand each other’s perspectives and motivations. These concepts highlight the common tendency to filter what we hear through our preconceived notions and biases, effectively blocking genuine understanding and connection. By recognizing and addressing this habit, I have learned to listen with an open heart and mind, allowing for more meaningful and unbiased communication. The ongoing coaching provided by Vivek has been instrumental in reinforcing these lessons, helping me to continuously reflect on and improve my listening skills.”

Taeko Suga, Assistant Development Officer

“Over the past year, I had the valuable opportunity to participate in an intensive staff development program that pushed me to step out of my comfort zone and improve my ability to express myself. This experience was both a challenge and an opportunity, as I worked to refine how I interact and connect with others, overcoming language and cultural barriers. Throughout this journey, my coach provided unwavering support, listening to my concerns and offering valuable advice. Although I am still refining these skills, this process has been an important step toward becoming a more effective communicator. I would especially like to thank Vivek, my coach.”
