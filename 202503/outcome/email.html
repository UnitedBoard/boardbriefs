Board Briefs
 
March 2025
 
 
 

Dear Trustees,

It is my pleasure to share with you the latest updates and highlights in this edition of Board Briefs. As we reflect on recent activities and look ahead to the opportunities before us, I want to begin by expressing my deepest gratitude for your continued dedication and engagement in advancing the mission of the United Board.

Last weekend, we convened an intensive two-day Board Meeting on strategic planning, attended by over 35 trustees (including trustees-elect), selected staff members, and Nygren Consulting. The gathering was a testament to the collective commitment of our community to shaping a clear and impactful vision for the future. Your insights, thoughtful contributions, and collaborative spirit during this process have been truly remarkable. I extend my heartfelt thanks to each of you for your active participation and to the chair and members of the Strategic Planning Steering Task Force for spearheading this critical initiative.

This Board Meeting marks a significant step forward as we chart a course that aligns with our shared aspirations for higher education in Asia. The data and ideas collected over the weekend will now be carefully analyzed by Dr. David Nygren and his team to form the basis for further discussions at the June Board Meeting in Thailand. I eagerly anticipate your ongoing support as we progress on this important journey together.

This edition also highlights several key initiatives that exemplify our mission in action. The Heads of Institution Leadership Strategy Institute, inaugurated at Singapore Management University last July, has begun to yield transformative results. Before the full institute was concluded with its last webinar in December participating leaders had already implemented innovative projects at their institutions with encouraging results. These efforts underscore the power of strategic leadership in driving meaningful change across higher education.

In Northeast India, we recently conducted a series of leadership programs, institutional visits, and workshops that brought together educators and administrators from hitherto underserved regions. Highlights included faculty development sessions in Shillong that equipped participants with tools for whole person education (WPE) and leadership workshops tailored to regional needs. Visits to institutions like Don Bosco College in Manipur further deepened our engagement with local communities, fostering collaboration and innovation.

Additionally, I am delighted to share that we recently hosted an alumni gathering in Chennai, which brought together 38 alumni from various United Board programs. This vibrant event not only reconnected alumni to us and each other but also fostered meaningful discussions on how their experiences have shaped their professional journeys. The gathering underscored the multiplying impact of our work through alumni networks and inspired renewed determination to advance whole person education across Asia.

Our February visits to Cambodia and Laos strengthened partnerships with institutions such as the Royal University of Phnom Penh and Souphanouvong University while exploring new opportunities for collaboration. These engagements illuminated both challenges—such as declining enrolment in Laos—and opportunities for leadership development and faculty capacity-building programs that align closely with our mission.

Finally, I encourage you to take note of the information provided regarding upcoming committee meetings as well as preparations for the June Board Meetings. I look forward to seeing you at the meetings.

Thank you once again for your unwavering support and commitment. Together, we are building a stronger foundation for whole person education and leadership development across Asia.

With gratitude and appreciation,

 
 

Pareena Gupta Lawrence
President
United Board for Christian Higher Education in Asia

Reflections on Staff Development
 
 

Ricky Cheng, Executive Vice President

“My experience with Open Consulting, guided by the exceptional consultants Vivek, Sangeeta, and Ruby, has been transformative in both my professional and personal life. Their approach to team dynamics and trust-building exercises has left a lasting impact on our organization and its staff. The sessions were meticulously designed to foster an environment where biases are set aside, and trust is built through genuine connection and understanding. Through engaging and interactive exercises such as ‘empathy walk’ and the concept of ‘downloading’, we were encouraged to look beyond interactions on the surface and truly understand each other’s perspectives and motivations. These concepts highlight the common tendency to filter what we hear through our preconceived notions and biases, effectively blocking genuine understanding and connection. By recognizing and addressing this habit, I have learned to listen with an open heart and mind, allowing for more meaningful and unbiased communication. The ongoing coaching provided by Vivek has been instrumental in reinforcing these lessons, helping me to continuously reflect on and improve my listening skills.”

Taeko Suga, Assistant Development Officer

“Over the past year, I had the valuable opportunity to participate in an intensive staff development program that pushed me to step out of my comfort zone and improve my ability to express myself. This experience was both a challenge and an opportunity, as I worked to refine how I interact and connect with others, overcoming language and cultural barriers. Throughout this journey, my coach provided unwavering support, listening to my concerns and offering valuable advice. Although I am still refining these skills, this process has been an important step toward becoming a more effective communicator. I would especially like to thank Vivek, my coach.”
