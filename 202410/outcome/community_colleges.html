October 2024
 
 
 
 
 
Trailblazing Community Colleges
 
 

After six years of strategic efforts, we achieved a significant milestone by creating educational pathways for marginalized youth in eastern and northern Sri Lanka. On September 25 and 26, Pareena visited Batticaloa, Sri Lanka, to inaugurate three community colleges: Vivekananda Community College, St. John's Community College (photo above), and Miani Community College (photo below).

The inauguration of these colleges signifies the successful implementation of our long-term commitment to support higher education in underserved regions. These fully operational institutions will serve as vital educational hubs for local youth, offering a variety of life skills and vocational training programs to enhance their employability and future prospects.

In Batticaloa, Pareena engaged with faculty members who had undergone training in India. These educators will form the backbone of the colleges. Her consultations highlighted an urgent need for additional training in several key areas, including mental health awareness and effective teaching strategies.

Looking ahead, we will continue to support the colleges by assisting in curriculum development to ensure alignment with the needs of their community. Additionally, we plan to organize faculty development programs focused on mental health, psychology, and advanced teaching methodologies. Furthermore, we aim to evaluate program effectiveness by assessing student success and community impact to refine our support strategies.
 
 

We are committed to deepening our collaboration to equip students from these regions with essential life skills. By empowering these students, we not only benefit them individually but also create a positive ripple effect within their families and communities.
