Board Briefs
 
October 2024
 
Distributing supplies to children at a service center of Vivekananda Community College, Sri Lanka
 
 

Dear Trustees,

As we step into October, I am delighted to share with you the latest developments and milestones of the United Board. Our mission to support Christian higher education across Asia continues to thrive through impactful initiatives, collaborations, and the unwavering dedication of our team and partners.

In September, I had the honor of attending the 34th Annual Scientific Meeting and Reunion of the Severance Alumni Association in Los Angeles. This gathering reaffirmed our longstanding partnership with Yonsei University, which has spanned over 70 years. The event provided an invaluable opportunity for networking, knowledge sharing, and discussing future endeavors. Severance alumni remain deeply committed to their alma mater as evidenced by the $6.7 million raised in support of Yonsei University’s College of Medicine last fiscal year.

We successfully completed the Heads of Institution Leadership Strategy Summit in Singapore in July. I was fortunate to participate in this impactful weeklong event together with 27 heads of institution from South and Southeast Asia. We continue our partnership with Ewha University, Scranton Women’s Leadership Center in South Korea, and the Wesley Foundation in Japan to develop and support the virtual program Women’s Leadership in Asia. More recently, we collaborated with the Centre of Holistic Teaching and Learning at Hong Kong Baptist University to launch a three-month-long online certificate course titled “Introduction to Digital Ethics", which attracted appropriately 500 registrants.

We also saw the successful conclusion of our English for Prime Movers (EPM) program, which brought together educators from Indonesia and Vietnam to strengthen their communication and advocacy skills. The hybrid format fostered not only professional growth but also a strong sense of community among the participants. Programs like EPM underscore our commitment to expanding the skillsets of educators for their continuous professional development.

A major highlight of this period was the inauguration of three community colleges in Batticaloa, Sri Lanka: Vivekananda, St. John's, and Miani Community Colleges. These colleges are now operational, offering vocational training and life skills programs that will enhance the employability of local youth. Our ongoing collaboration with these institutions will focus on aligning their curricula with community needs and empowering students to be agents of change in their communities, in areas that have been ravaged by disasters.

Our exploratory visit to Northeast India highlighted the needs of this region and further demonstrated our dedication to building partnerships and providing capacity building programs in this culturally rich but academically underserved region. The meetings with faculty, students, and educational leaders were both insightful and inspiring. We look forward to deepening our engagement in this region and establishing long-term collaborations that will benefit both students and the institutions.

Another significant step in our staff development efforts is the integration of Microsoft Copilot AI into our daily operations. This AI tool will enhance productivity across our offices by streamlining tasks, improving data analysis, and fostering greater collaboration. Our colleagues have embraced this new technology, and we expect it to play a pivotal role in advancing our mission in the coming years.

Good technology is necessary, but great people are essential; thus, staff development remains a high priority for us. On this note, I am pleased to announce the promotion of Ms. Cynthia Yuen to Senior Program Officer. Cynthia has been a vital member of our program team for nearly seven years, and her promotion is a testament to her dedication and contributions. We are also excited to welcome Dr. Bo Bo Lwin, who will serve as a program officer and bring a wealth of experience in leadership development programs. Their contributions will be integral as we continue to grow and evolve.

As we move forward, let us continue to embrace these opportunities for growth, collaboration, and innovation. Together we are making meaningful contributions to the communities we serve, ensuring a brighter future for higher education in Asia.

Remember that October and November are busy times for committees and Board meetings. Please note the dates and times of the various meetings at the end of the newsletter.

As always, thank you for your ongoing support and dedication to the United Board's mission.
 

Pareena Gupta Lawrence
President, United Board for Christian Higher Education in Asia

Staffing News
 
 

Promotion of Ms. Cynthia Yuen

We are pleased that Ms. Cynthia Yuen has been promoted to Senior Program Officer, effective this fiscal year. Cynthia has been a valuable member of the program team for nearly seven years. She has consistently demonstrated a high level of professionalism, hard work, and dedication to the mission of the United Board.

Cynthia is well-respected. Her candor, friendliness, and willingness to provide her views and suggestions for improving our operation are greatly appreciated by her colleagues. Scholars and administrators at other institutions have always found Cynthia to be a source of good advice and timely assistance.

Cynthia's promotion is a testament to her exceptional performance and the positive impact she has made on the United Board. In her new role, Cynthia will take on additional responsibilities of managing new initiatives, while continuing to support programs in Northeast Asia and faculty development and scholarships.

 
 
 

New Staff
Dr. Bo Bo Lwin
Program Officer

We are thrilled to announce that Dr. Bo Bo Lwin will join the United Board as the Program Officer for our Leadership Development Programs. Dr. Lwin brings a wealth of experience in education for sustainable development, leadership training, and community empowerment. His impressive track record includes founding a respected NGO in Myanmar and pioneering innovative educational platforms to support students affected by civil unrest. Dr. Lwin's commitment to participatory research and his dedication to ethical living and learning make him an exceptional addition to our team.

Dr. Lwin holds a Doctorate in Higher Education for Sustainable Development from The Open University, UK. His research focused on integrating sustainable development into higher education in Myanmar. He has a strong track record in strategic planning, project management, and fostering collaboration. Dr. Lwin's leadership skills and ability to build relationships will be invaluable as he manages our leadership development programs.
