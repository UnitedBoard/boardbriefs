Board Briefs
 
August 2024
 
 
 
 
A Tour to Taiwan
 
 

From 27 May to 31 May, 2024, Pareena, Ricky and Jakarin visited several universities within the United Board network in Taiwan. This visit aimed to strengthen relationships, understand challenges, and explore collaboration opportunities with these long-time partners. They also met with friends of the United Board in Taiwan and visited a prominent Buddhist organization.
 
Chang Jung Christian University (CJCU) in Tainan on 27 May
 

The delegation was warmly welcomed and engaged in discussions focusing on whole person education, leadership development, and collaboration within the Christian higher education community. The dedication to academic excellence and the vibrant student community at CJCU left a lasting impression. Discussions with senior administrators provided valuable insights into innovative research and educational practices. The visit underscored the strong partnership between CJCU and the United Board, highlighting the transformative impact of United Board-supported programs on personal and professional growth. Potential collaborations in faculty development and research partnerships were eagerly anticipated.
 
Tunghai University in Taichung on 28 May
 

The day began with breakfast at the residence of Dr. Juanelva Rose, a long-time friend and now a donor, followed by a campus tour and meeting with senior academic leaders and staff. The discussions covered various topics related to education, focusing particularly on the liberal arts education system, the introduction of an honors program, and the expansion of international college programs. Challenges in promoting liberal arts education, the significance of English language instruction, and potential revenue generation from adult and executive education programs were also addressed. The delegation also visited Congdon House and discussed the possibility of Tunghai University hosting the Asian Academy of Campus Ministry (AACM) in 2025.
 
Fu Jen Catholic University in Taipei on 29 May
 

They attended the International Community Service program ceremony and engaged in meaningful conversations with university leaders about holistic education, leadership development, and the impact of AI on education. The campus tour included the Office of University History, the Immaculate Conception Chapel, and the Fu Jen Catholic University Hospital. The visit highlighted Fu Jen Catholic University's commitment to excellence, compassion, and faith. Discussions emphasized internationalization, strategic collaborations, and adapting to technological advancements.
 
Soochow University in Taipei on 30 May
 

They were welcomed by President Chyan-Long Jan and his team. The discussions focused on innovative initiatives and potential collaborations. Topics included leadership and faculty development, challenges faced by private universities, and fundraising strategies. The visit highlighted the importance of building capacity among faculty and administrators, addressing financial challenges, and engaging alumni, in particular, Soochow alumni in the US, for long-term support.

In addition to university visits, the delegation met with Mr. Paul Chan, a donor, at the American Club in Taipei on 29 May. The meeting focused on the ongoing work of the United Board and the impact of Mr. Chan's contributions. The delegation also had a productive dinner with Ching Mai Wu, a former chair of the board and a trustee of Tunghai University, discussing the ongoing partnership and the Congdon House renovation project. On 30 May, they had a dinner with Prof. Haydn Chen, former president of Tunghai University and Chief Strategy Officer of National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University, discussing Taiwan's education landscape and the importance of whole person education.
 
 

The trip concluded with a visit to the Tzu Chi Foundation, a Taiwanese humanitarian organization founded in 1966 by the Buddhist nun Cheng Yen, in Hualien on 31 May. The delegation learned about the foundation's wide-ranging initiatives in medical care, education, environmental sustainability, and community development. The visit highlighted the foundation's commitment to compassion and selflessness, which serves as the cornerstone of their service-oriented approach.
 
 

The visits to the four universities covered a wide range of topics and initiatives. We went into great depth in the discussions of partnerships and collaborations, leadership development and faculty support. In all, the Taiwan visit was a significant step towards strengthening partnerships, understanding challenges, and exploring collaboration opportunities with our partners in Taiwan. The dialogues with our partners reaffirmed our mutual commitment to addressing challenges and fostering collaboration, innovation, and knowledge-sharing.
