Board Briefs
 
August 2024
 
 
 

Dear Trustees,

I am delighted to share with you the latest edition of Board Briefs, which highlights the vibrant and impactful activities of the United Board over the recent months.

Our June Board Meeting and Study Trip to Yogyakarta, Indonesia, provided invaluable opportunities for connection and collaboration. Your active engagement in the program was instrumental to its success, and I thank you for your participation. The experiences we shared, the friendships we forged, and the insights we gained will undoubtedly leave a lasting impression. To relive these moments, please enjoy the photographs included in this edition. For a closer look at the overwhelmingly positive feedback and some very insightful comments we received through the Board Meeting survey, click here.

Following the Board Meeting, our team participated in a staff retreat where we focused on development exercises, team-building activities, and strategic planning. This gathering provided a valuable opportunity for the United Board Team to reflect on our collective experiences, celebrate milestones, and strategize for the future. This ongoing spirit of collaboration and strategic dialogue is strengthening the foundation upon which we are advancing our mission.

In this issue, you will also find a detailed report on my visits in May to several universities in Taiwan. These engagements with university leaders revolved around whole person education, leadership development, and exploring potential new partnerships. These institutions showcased a strong commitment to academic excellence and vibrant student communities, reaffirming the strength of our partnerships in Taiwan.

In May, the Asian Academy of Campus Ministry (AACM) organized an exposure trip to Indonesia for campus ministers, focused on fostering a deeper understanding of multi-faith and cross-cultural perspectives. This valuable experience will significantly enhance our efforts to promote student well-being and create enriched learning environments across Asia.

We also marked the 20th anniversary of the Institute for Advanced Study in Asian Cultures and Theologies (IASACT) in July. This celebration coincided with the IASACT 2024 four-week residential program, themed "The Poetics, Politics, and Performances of Space". The program's success is a testament to our ongoing commitment to advancing theological education and research.

Finally, I am pleased to welcome Dr. Anita Christine Tiphagne as our new Consultant, Assistant Director of Administration and Partnerships. Dr. Tiphagne's extensive experience and commitment to our mission are critical as we continue to strengthen and expand our network partnerships.

Looking forward, I am optimistic and grateful for the collective efforts of our Board members, staff, and our network. Together, we are making strides in advancing whole person education and promoting meaningful collaborations across Asia. I hope you enjoy reading about our recent activities and feel inspired to continue your support for our mission with unwavering enthusiasm and dedication.
 

Pareena Gupta Lawrence
President, United Board for Christian Higher Education in Asia

A Journey of Multi-faith Exposure and Discovery
 
 

The Asian Academy of Campus Ministry (AACM) exposure trip took place during the period 1-10 May 2024. This trip aimed to empower university campus ministers with a deeper understanding of multi-faith campus ministry in a diverse context. The program's objectives included fostering interfaith dialogue, exploring multi-faith practices, and enhancing skills in promoting religious tolerance and inclusivity. A total of 29 chaplains, campus ministers, academics, and administrators from seven countries immersed themselves in the multi-faith learning experience.

The trip commenced at Universitas Kristen Duta Wacana (UKDW) in Yogyakarta, Indonesia, and included panel discussions and visits to various universities, where participants explored interfaith models and engaged in meaningful dialogue with local communities.
