Board Briefs
 
January 2025
 
 
 
 
Whole Person Goes from Strength to Strength
 
 

The Whole Person Education Academy (WPEA) concluded its week-long onsite program on 26 October 2024, at the Gokongwei Brothers School of Education and Learning Design at Ateneo de Manila University in the Philippines. This year’s program brought together 33 participants from 12 higher education institutions across India, Indonesia, Laos, Myanmar, and the Philippines. These scholars and university administrators returned to their home institutions not only as trained educators but also as advocates for whole person education, equipped to inspire meaningful change within their academic communities.
 
 

The Academy offered participants a forum for in-depth discussions on crucial themes including spirituality, inclusivity, and the impact of technology on the humanities. Guided by experts from the Ateneo Teacher Center, participants explored innovative approaches to cultivating a sense of belonging and discernment in education. Through a thoughtfully designed curriculum encompassing lectures, collaborative activities, and reflective discussions, they thoroughly explored the principles of holistic learning. The program encouraged them to reflect on their roles as educators and develop actionable strategies for implementing whole person education at their respective institutions.

Building upon feedback from the previous year, WPEA 2024 incorporated several significant improvements. We refined session content to enhance focus, with smaller group presentations that allowed for more meaningful engagement. Structured creativity exercises were expanded to include reflective components, and practical toolkits were provided to equip participants with actionable resources for implementation. Despite disruptions that were caused by Typhoon Kristine on the fourth day of the Academy, the organizers successfully adapted by introducing alternative activities such as Pecha Kucha demonstrations, ensuring that all participants continued to have a valuable learning experience.

Participants were deeply appreciative for the program's quality and impact. Homer C. Balmes, Dean of Students at Trinity University of Asia, noted how the WPEA aligned with his university’s strategic planning efforts and he expressed his intention to integrate whole person education principles into student affairs. Dr. Alphy Geever, Assistant Professor at St. Stephen’s College in India, commended the modules for addressing students’ holistic development. Similarly, Jeffrey Aguila, Assistant Professor at Silliman University, emphasized the adaptability of best practices learned during WPEA for his institution.
 
 

Rita Atienza, Director of the Ateneo Teacher Center and a key figure in WPEA’s development, outlined her vision for future program iterations. She proposed extending onsite sessions to foster deeper interaction and networking among participants while minimizing reliance on online components. Additionally, she suggested developing supplementary materials such as concise pamphlets and video-based resources to enrich existing content. She also proposed expanding speaker diversity by including educators from other Philippine institutions to enhance the program’s inclusivity and broaden its scope.

The Whole Person Education Academy continues to demonstrate a commitment to creating educational environments that value students as individuals with unique potential. WPEA 2024 serves as a testament to the transformative power of whole person education in cultivating future-ready learners who can contribute meaningfully to society.
