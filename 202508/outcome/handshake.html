Firmer than a Handshake
Three partner institutions enter into deeper collaborative agreements
 
 

Recently, two Memoranda of Understanding were signed, marking meaningful steps in our network’s partnership-building journey. On 21 July, St. Christopher’s College of Education, Chennai, and Ateneo de Davao University, Philippines, signed an agreement that highlights their mutual commitment to promoting international academic cooperation, global learning, and educational empowerment for both faculty and students. Likewise, Stella Maris College, Chennai, has established a similar partnership with Ateneo de Davao University. This signals a shared vision and a strong eagerness among our partners to explore new learning opportunities beyond national boundaries.

These MOUs reflect a thoughtful, needs-based approach. The discussions leading to these agreements were facilitated by United Board staff, utilizing a repository of information about partner institutions. Through this system, institutions could indicate their interests in specific collaborative areas, enabling effective identification and matching of needs and strengths among the partners.

It is important to note that each partnership will be managed through designated coordinating offices at the respective institutions, ensuring dedicated oversight and effective implementation. The agreements have been crafted with flexibility and sustainability in mind, providing for renewal and regular review so they can adapt to future needs.

These new collaborations exemplify the United Board’s commitment to building a vibrant ecosystem within our network where institutions can forge authentic partnerships and grow together strategically to enhance their curricular and co-curricular opportunities. This initiative isn’t just about cooperation; it’s about co-creation and shared purpose. We are confident that these MOUs will generate substantive results, fostering innovation and mutual growth among our network members, and serving as a model for future partnership initiatives across Asia.

We look forward to sustaining this momentum and witnessing the positive outcomes these partnerships will bring to our collective effort to transform higher education in the region.
