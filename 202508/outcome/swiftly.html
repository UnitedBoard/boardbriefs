The United Board Spirit Powers Forward
Pareena Gupta Lawrence
 
 

As previously stated in our email updates, I am pleased to provide this recap of the progress and current status of the Student Emergency Scholarship Institutional Grant Fund.

In June 2025, following Board approval, we officially launched the fund to assist displaced students affected by reductions in international aid. Collaborations with key institutions, most notably the Institute of International Education (IIE), have been crucial in identifying eligible students and managing the grant application and disbursement process.

To date, the United Board has committed approximately USD 301,375 over two years to support 26 students across four institutions in Thailand and the Philippines. Scholarships range from 1â€“1.5 years for graduate students and up to 2 years for undergraduates. We are encouraged to see that each institution has also demonstrated its commitment by contributing financially to support its students.

While the total amount disbursed is lower than initially projected, we have been able to strengthen our longstanding relationship with the Harvard-Yenching Institute as they stepped forward to support 17 students at Mizoram University and the American University of Phnom Penh, two institutions outside our direct network. Together, we met the needs of all displaced students physically attending universities who were left without support, as identified by IIE.
