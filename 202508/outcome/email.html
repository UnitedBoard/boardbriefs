Board Briefs
 
August 2025
 
 
 

Dear Trustees,

Warm greetings! I write with deep gratitude for your steadfast guidance and to present this edition of Board Briefs—a vibrant reflection of the energy, collaboration, and impact that your leadership enables across our network.

We begin with vivid recollections from our summer Board Meeting and Study Trip in Thailand. The clickable photo boxes will transport you back to Bangkok and Chiang Mai, where strategic planning sessions, board business discussions, the celebrations of our graduating United Board Fellows, meaningful exchanges at the Presidents' Forum at Payap University, cultural visits, and shared meals all underscore the values and the mission that unite us. These moments of fellowship and heartfelt farewells to departing Board members remind us of the strength and spirit of our community.

Next, you’ll find an update on the Student Emergency Scholarship Institutional Grant Fund—an initiative both timely and essential in safeguarding educational continuity for displaced students. Thanks to your support and decisive action, 26 students across four institutions in Thailand and the Philippines can continue their studies until graduation. This is a powerful testament to our collective responsiveness in moments of need. Additionally, the Harvard-Yenching Institute’s generous assistance in joining us in this effort stands as a heartening development.

Dr. David Johns, a seasoned academic and university leader, offers an external perspective on two of our flagship programs—IASACT in Hong Kong and the Fellows Program in Singapore. His reflections on how space, intention, and community are shaping a new generation of reflective leaders, along with his immersive participation in both programs, have provided invaluable insights that will inform the future development of these programs.

Our commitment to building partnerships continues to strengthen through recently signed Memoranda of Understanding connecting institutions in India and the Philippines. These collaborative agreements exemplify genuine partnership development within our network, made possible by the dedicated efforts of Dr. Anita Tiphagne, whose diligent work and comprehensive repository of institutional information facilitated these connections.

A particularly exciting milestone is the overwhelmingly positive response to the launch of our new AI webinar series, which drew over 250 participants from across Asia. Led by our talented Mr. Christian Aung, the webinar invited our alumni to play a leading role in building a robust future where AI is thoughtfully integrated into higher education. This five-part series will continue to meet the strong demand for meaningful engagement with emerging technologies while upholding our commitment to holistic, ethical, and spiritually grounded education.

I am also delighted to welcome three distinguished individuals to the Board—Dr. Eun Mee Kim, Rev. Dr. Huang Po Ho, and Dr. Sheila Amin. Their expertise in university leadership, theology, and public policy will enrich our deliberations and broaden our collective vision.

Finally, please mark your calendars for our special Board Meeting on strategic planning, scheduled for September 5 (US) / September 6 (Asia). A complete schedule by time zone, along with Zoom credentials, is included at the end of this issue for your convenience.

Thank you for the wisdom, resources, and care you bring to our shared mission. May the stories that follow reaffirm the profound difference your support and leadership make in the lives of students, faculty, and institutions across Asia.

 

With deep appreciation and warmest regards,

 
 

Pareena Gupta Lawrence
President
United Board for Christian Higher Education in Asia
