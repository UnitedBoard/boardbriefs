Board Briefs
 
August 2025
 
 
 
 
 
Two Programs, One Purpose
IASACT and Fellows Program in the eyes of David Johns, Ph.D.
 
 

David taught theology and ethics for many years in the United States; he has held senior leadership roles in higher education, including serving as a vice president for academics, college president, and since 2023, has worked as a consultant with the United Board. During the summer of 2025, he participated in both the IASACT and the United Board Fellows programs as an external assessor.

In the span of a few weeks this summer, two of the United Board’s flagship programs took place in two vibrant hubs of Asian higher education—one in Hong Kong, the other in Singapore. Though distinct in format and audience, the Institute for Advanced Study in Asian Cultures and Theologies (IASACT) and the United Board Fellows Program both reveal the power of space, intention, and community to form leaders in ways that are enduring and transformative.
 
 
 

IASACT, hosted at the Divinity School of Chung Chi College at the Chinese University of Hong Kong, remains a jewel of the United Board’s mission. It gathers early to mid-career scholars from across Asia into a month-long residency of research, reflection, and intercultural dialogue. The experience is intensely personal yet profoundly communal: early morning writing, shared meals, theological inquiry, and evenings of unplanned conversation that often stretch long after the session’s end. I was surprised by how quickly this group bonded and became a genuine community—a testament to the program’s structure and spirit. Participants spoke frequently of vocational clarity, renewed purpose, and scholarly direction rediscovered.

IASACT’s strength lies in its structure—space to pause, to think deeply, and to build relationships across institutions, faith traditions, and academic disciplines. Its impact is not only measurable in publications and professional development, but in friendships that transcend borders and in the realization that one’s work can be both deeply contextual and broadly relevant.
 
 
 

The United Board Fellows Program, by contrast, is a year-long program designed for those advancing in administrative leadership roles. The opening week, held at Singapore Management University, introduced the 2025-26 cohort of university administrators and faculty leaders to a rich set of ideas and challenges—governance, change management, strategy, team building, and persuasion. A highlight of the week was a full day focused on the United Board’s mission and dynamic vision of higher education. Presentations on whole person education, fundraising, spiritual practices, and leadership as vocation anchored the week in the values that mark the United Board as distinct from other leadership training programs.

Across both programs, a shared theme emerged: leadership grounded in faith, shaped by reflection, and sustained through community. While their content and cadence differ, both IASACT and the Fellows Program serve as bridges linking institutions, cultures, and individuals to a larger vision of Asian higher education. That is work worth doing!

I was privileged to participate in both gatherings this year and was reminded that the work of formation doesn’t always happen in classrooms or lecture halls. It happens in the pauses between sessions, over tea near the Merlion in Singapore, during shared laughter eating street food in Hong Kong, and in the realization that another person’s story resonates with one’s own. These are the moments that form the foundation of durable leadership and faithful scholarship.
 
 

Both programs are thriving—and both stand at a moment of opportunity. Their continued relevance will depend on subtle, strategic adjustments that respond to the changing needs of our partner institutions and the shifting landscape of higher education. But their core strength—the creation of thoughtful, reflective, values-driven leaders—remains as strong as ever.
