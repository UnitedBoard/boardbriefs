Board Briefs
 
October 2025
 
 
 

Dear Trustees,

It is with deep appreciation that I share this newest edition of Board Briefs, which captures the energy, purpose, and faith-filled collaboration that define our shared mission since the last brief. It is a truthful reminder of how your leadership and partnership help our network flourish — from deepening connections across regions to advancing new forms of education and dialogue anchored in compassion and integrity.

You will be delighted to take a vivid look at how our Institutional Seed Grants are transforming colleges and universities in underserved regions in India and Sri Lanka. Programs at Jayaraj Annapackiam College for Women, Don Bosco College, and Patna Women’s College, among others, are fostering leadership and creativity among faculty, embedding holistic values into teaching and learning. These initiatives show that Whole Person Education flourishes not as a single model, but as a living ethos—one that empowers educators to create compassionate, inclusive, and innovative communities of learning rooted in local contexts.

Ethical leadership and innovation bring to light our alumni community’s growing engagement with the moral dimensions of technology. The second in our Alumni Webinar Series on the Use of AI in Higher Education, led by Dr. Rachel Siow Robertson of Hong Kong Baptist University, explored how Christian higher education can shape the ethical use of artificial intelligence through vision, virtue, and responsibility. With overwhelmingly enthusiastic responses from alumni and other participants and another session scheduled this November, this series exemplifies how our community continues to lead conversations that unite innovation with integrity.

In September, we celebrated a unique collaboration with the Museum of Chinese in America in New York, where donor and author Dr. Robin T. W. Yuan and his daughter shared from his novel Red Bishop. Their reflections on vocation and sacrifice remind us that the values nurtured by early Christian institutions in China continue to bear fruit in succeeding generations. This story invites us to remember that the United Board’s work is not only an investment in the present but also a bridge across time—a testament to faith, memory, and the ongoing legacy of Christian education.

Lastly, I have reflected on my meaningful engagements in South Korea, the US, the Philippines, and China—each encounter rekindling the friendships and partnerships that form the heart of the United Board’s work. From the plenary session presentation at Yonsei University’s 140th anniversary to conversations on leadership at Ewha and Seoul Women’s Universities, these visits highlighted our legacy of collaboration and the promise of renewed connections, while in the US, reconnecting with trustees, alumni, and friends reminded me of the intergenerational bonds that sustain our mission. Together, these journeys and encounters illustrate how the power of relationship continues to shape our hope-filled work in Christian higher education.

I look forward to welcoming you to our upcoming Board Meetings in early November, where we will gather once again to discern together, put the finishing touches on the strategic plan, and chart the path ahead for the United Board’s next chapter of service and growth.

 

With gratitude and warm regards,

 
 

Pareena Gupta Lawrence
President
United Board for Christian Higher Education in Asia
